Angel M. Osorio, J.D. (Angel) is a Camden native, has a Bachelor of Arts degree in English, a Juris Doctorate degree, and years of experience in community outreach, civic engagement, legislation, urban renewal, crime prevention, youth programming and curriculum development. Angel also has experience in developing policies and providing training in schools for community service projects, discipline and bullying, and has taught civics lessons on all levels from elementary school to college, and in the Camden County Detention Facility. She is currently an Adjunct Professor at Rutgers University, teaching an Urban Studies Practicum that includes a community service requisite.

Angel is the Chief Executive Officer of a non-profit in Camden (the District Council Collaborative Board/the DCCBs), which concentrates on improving conditions in the city. Much of the DCCB’s work centers on encouraging civic engagement, improving public safety and diverting individuals away from the criminal justice system. She has years of experience in crime prevention, technological advancements in policing, reduction of juvenile delinquency and recidivism, social service referrals, employment, designing mechanisms to prevent substance abuse, developing community-policing strategies that help to connect law enforcement to the community and to heighten accountability, and make neighborhoods safer.

Angel is a member of the Camden County Workforce Investment’s Youth Council, a board member for The Work Group, which is a New Jersey Youth Corps program serving at-risk teenagers who have dropped out of high school, and is a member of Young Urban Leaders. She is the Chair of the Arts Council for Rutgers’ University’s Center for the Arts and a member of the Law School’s Pro Bono committee. In addition, Angel is the Co-Chair of the Camden County Local Advisory Council on Alcohol and Drug Abuse (LACADA) and is certified in community outreach and Crime Prevention through Environmental Design.

Mary Ann Settembrino has over twenty-five years of experience facilitating more than a thousand individuals and organizations to help maximize their personal and professional potential.

Mary Ann assists people that are truly committed to achieving the success and fulfillment they desire, to obtain it more readily through proven strategies that work . She believes “ that we live in a world that demands results - not excuses."

Her mastery of the “science” of communication and the established methodologies that are available to people for producing prompt and sustainable results enables her to teach clients specific strategies they can use to remove personal obstacles and achieve their personal goals.

She is a graduate of Anthony Robbins Mastery University, an intensive program taught by some of the most accomplished business teachers and “doers” in the world. Mastery University provides a method of learning from various masters in their fields about their strategies for success and their own experiences with failure and life’s learning lessons; saving many from decades of trial and error experiences in attempting to achieve the results they want. Impressed by the results from the Robbin’s programs, Ms. Settembrino pursued further studies with Dr. Clare Graves work with “Spiral Dynamics." This methodology uses a psychosocial scale to ascertain the belief system an individual uses so one can better predict that individual’s behavior. She further studied the neurotechnology on which much of the Tony Robbins programs are based, which led to two Master certificates of Neuro Linguistics and Neuro Strategies and also two Master certificates of Hypno-Therapy. Mary Ann also worked for Anthony Robbins as an executive coach in his partnership with Chet Holmes. Mary Ann Settembrino is also a physician of Chinese medicine who specializes in integrated and functional medicine. She and her husband started one of the first integrated medical centers in Philadelphia in 1995.

Mary Ann joins the Camden Leadership Academy as an Advisory Board Member and a Life Coach Instructor focusing on the Academy’s Personal Life Skills training sessions, which she has helped develop and organize. Mary Ann also teaches the classroom sessions to students while training future instructors on how to teach the program.

Al Pierce was born and raised in the City of Camden and is a business executive with decades of experience managing and operating OEM businesses that have taken him around the globe. From his travels he has experienced many different types of communities, economies and lifestyles of people from around the world. He is passionate about the strengths and abilities of the individual and how these skill sets and personal beliefs help shape the lives and the communities we all live in.


Al Pierce has been designing custom sporting goods products and selling them throughout the United States for decades. He began creating and manufacturing protective sports equipment for his own start up company in the late eighties. Playing college football at the University of Arizona, he took notice of the business of sports and sporting goods. After college he designed a series of products that featured nearly 2 dozen patented and trademarked items for a number of sports including football, basketball, baseball, softball, soccer and others. Overtime, he sourced 100’s of manufacturing facilities throughout Asia, the Far East and South America.

Al also oversaw the sales of his unique sporting goods products along with a variety of professional sports licensed items which included NFL, MLB, NASCAR and COLLEGIATE licensed sports products. Customers included national and regional retail chains along with a majority of the major professional sports teams. Many items enjoyed national exposure among NFL teams and top professional athletes, Collegiate teams and several Olympic teams. Retail customers included mass merchants such as Walmart, Target, Kmart and Toys R US and regional and national sporting goods chains such as Modell’s, Academy, Big 5, MC Sports and Dicks Sporting Goods.

As the result of being the founder and owner of a small multi-million dollar business, Mr. Pierce acquired a wealth of experience managing all aspects of business operations. He is perhaps best known for his sales and marketing capabilities in addition to being known as a creative designer, a problem solver, and a leader that thinks outside the box.

These various business experiences and world travels have taught Mr. Pierce much about how different cultures and various businesses operate and work. All having the common denominator of personal growth through self reliance and self awareness, along with perpetual improvement. Traits he hopes to bring to the Camden Leadership Academy.
